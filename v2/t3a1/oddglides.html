Introduction

The glide of a number is defined as the number of iterations of the equation that are required before a number smaller than the original is reached. Glide records are set when a number's glide is larger than those of all the numbers that come before it. A list of records can be found at http://personal.computrain.nl/eric/wondrous/. I've been calculating the "Odd Glides", essentially the same property, but with the even numbers removed. At least for the first million numbers the two sets are the same:
