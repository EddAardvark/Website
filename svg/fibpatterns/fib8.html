The Reduced Pattern

Because 8 is a multiple of 2, and the series generated include multiples of those contained in the smaller patterns. The 4 squares in the N := :2 pattern can be seen duplicated in the N := :8 pattern: at (0, 0), (0, 4), (4, 0) and (4, 4). The 16 squares in the N := :4 pattern can also be found: at (0,0), (2,0), (4,0), etc. The pattern in the fourth column shows the original pattern with these cells removed (transparent). You can also see the N := :2 pattern in the N := :4 one.
